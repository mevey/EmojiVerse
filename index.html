Discover the evolution of the fastest growing "language" in history – as well as the meaning and usage of these characters today.



At right is real-time use of a few emoji on Twitter, according to EmojiTracker. "Face with tears of joy" is the most popular emoji, used 300 times per second.

But where did emoji come from?

Originally meaning pictograph, the word emoji comes from Japanese: e (絵, "picture") + moji (文字, "character"). Today, emoji are a complex network of pictorial symbols that can be used in text – either as a standalone character, with a modifier for skin tone or gender, or in a sequence, with a "zero width joiner" (or ZWJ, pronounced “zwidge”). Read on for a history of how these characters have evolved over time.

  • 1999 Shigetaka Kurita creates emoji, a simple set of 176 “picture characters.” Meant to be sent alongside text characters, these 12×12 pixel monochrome icons are inspired by descriptive symbols of Japanese TV weather forecasts. ☀️
  • 2010 📈 +1006 emoji

    Emoji character sets are first incorporated into Unicode, a system for indexing characters. Standardization allows emojis to be used across different operating systems and by people around the world. 🌎
  • 2011 Apple adds an emoji keyboard to iPhones and other iOS devices, propelling more widespread adoption. 📱
  • 2012 📈 +13 emoji

    Apple's iOS 6 update includes new emoji for gay and lesbian couples, implied by same-sex characters that are holding hands. 🏳️‍🌈
  • 2013 Android follows Apple's lead, supporting emoji on their mobile devices as well. 🤖 The word "emoji" is also added to the Oxford English Dictionary. 📖
  • 2014 📈 +105 emoji

    Established by Jeremy Burge of Emojipedia, the first World Emoji Day takes place on July 17: the date is chosen because it appears on the iOS "calendar" emoji. (This is actually the date on which Apple introduced iCal, its calendar application, back in 2002.) 📅
  • 2015 📈 +361 emoji

    Unicode releases 5 symbol modifiers to provide a range of skin tones for emoji, based on the Fitzpatrick scale for classifying human skin color. Human emoji without a modifier should be displayed in a generic, non-realistic skin tone, such as bright yellow, blue, or gray. ✌️ Oxford Dictionary also names the "face with tears of joy" emoji as the word of the year. 😂
  • 2016 📈 +765 emoji

    Unicode improves gender diversity with its latest release, featuring new emoji of females as athletes and various other professions. ♀️ Apple also announces that the gun emoji will be changed from a realistic revolver to a water pistol. 🔫 The original collection of 176 emoji is acquired by Museum of Modern Art. 🖼️
  • 2017 📈 +239 emoji

    Researchers from the National Archives in Trencin, Slovakia claim to find the world's oldest original emoji: it's a smiley face drawn by a lawyer named Jan Ladislaides dating from 1635. 🔍 After 2 years of advocacy, activist Jennifer 8. Lee sees her petition for the dumpling emoji manifest on devices worldwide and continues to petition inclusive representation as founder of EmojiNation. 🥟
  • 2018 📈 +161 emoji

    A group of advocates including the dating app Tinder and Alexis Ohanian – Reddit co-founder and husband of Serena Williams – petition to support emoji representation of interracial couples. 🔥 Apple also proposes 13 emojis to better represent living with various disabilities, including a prosthetic arm and a service dog. 🐕

Meaning and Usage

Emoji Reader and Writer Sentiment

“Because emoji characters are treated as pictographs, they are encoded in Unicode based primarily on their general appearance, not on an intended semantic. In fact, when used as emoji, many of these characters acquire multiple meanings based on their appearance; for example, an emoji character for “bank” which includes the letters “BK” has taken on in Japan the secondary meaning “bakkureru” (a slang term for evading one’s responsibilities). The meaning of each emoji may vary depending on language, culture, and context.”
