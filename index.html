So, do emoji help us communicate? üí¨

Researchers have found that about 25% of the time, people disagree about whether the emotion being conveyed by an emoji is positive, negative, or neutral ‚Äì even when they are viewing the same emoji on the same type of device. Yet, examining Jose Berengueres' emoji sentiment dataset below, we do see a general correlation between sentiment of writers and readers of emoji: the symbols enable empathy, with negative emoji in the bottom left, and more positive, celebratory emoji in the top right.

"It's not surprising that a pictographic script like emoji has stepped in to fill those gaps ‚Äî it's flexible, immediate, and infuses tone beautifully."

How do I submit an emoji proposal? üí°

Encoded emoji are selected based on the following factors: (1) high expected usage, (2) distinctiveness, and (3) broad scope, or "useful ambiguity." Note that the overall process of submitting an emoji to seeing it on your device takes at least a year ‚Äì probably even longer.

Proposals are first submitted to the emoji subcommittee, who then forwards reasonable proposals to the Unicode Technical Committee for voting. But even after being approved by vote, proposals must be considered "highest priority" in order to be developed ‚Äì first by Unicode, then by platforms like Apple and Google ‚Äì to appear on computers and mobile phones. If you're still interested, get started by checking out the submission form template and FAQ.


What is the future of emoji? üîÆ

As the number of encoded emoji continues to grow, you may already experience the struggle to find the right symbol quickly. The Unicode Consortium currently anticipates a 60 character "emoji budget" per year, until alternative solutions come into play. Such longer-term solutions include supporting embedded graphics (a.k.a. "stickers"), since they aren't dependent on encoding: this would allow both faster implementation and much more freedom of expression.
